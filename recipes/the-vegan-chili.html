The Vegan Chili: an explosion of flavor


The Vegan Chili is the only chili you'll ever need! Forget the European versions with cheap ground pork and canned corn, which end up tasting like nothing.
This chili, which contains no meat or substitutes, is a pure explosion of flavor in your mouth that you'll rarely have experienced before.
The main ingredients, besides the many spices, are beans of your choice, chopped or peeled tomatoes, onions, lots of garlic, vegetable broth,
cilantro and, depending on how spicy you like it, jalapeños. You can let your creativity run wild with the toppings, or use leftovers.
I recommend vegan cold cream to balance out the spiciness and heat, chopped red onions or spring onions, and parsley. Round it off with a lime to add a little fresh acidity.
The only downside to this dish is that it doesn't cook quickly. Start early and be prepared to stir a lot! But before you bounce from the recipe: the preparation is super quick.


Ingredients:

For the chili:

  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 very large yellow onion, diced
  • 6 cloves garlic, chopped finely
  • 2 jalapeños, diced (remove membranes for less heat)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 4 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 ½ teaspoons smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • 3/4 cup(180 mL) dry red wine
  • 2 cups (480 mL) vegetable broth
  • 3 cans (425g-500g) beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder (Without sugar! You can find them in the baking section)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 ½ tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 cans (425-500g) whole peeled or crushed tomatoes. If whole: crush them by hand
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt plus more as needed
  • 1 tablespoon pure maple syrup, plus more to finish as needed
  • 1 to 1 1/2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup (12g) cilantro leaves and tender stems, chopped
  • Black pepper to taste

For the topping:

  • Vegan sour cream or diced avocado
  • Sliced scallions or chopped cilantro
  • Two halved limes
  • Chopped red onions
  • Any kind of bread or bread roll

How to make The Vegan Chili:

  1. Heat a large pot or a deep pan with a lid over medium to high heat. Add the olive oil and, once it starts to shimmer, add the onions and season them with a few pinches of salt. Cook for about 10 minutes,
    stirring frequently, until the onions turn nicely golden. If they begin to brown too quickly around the edges, stir more often and or add a small splash of water.

  2. Add the garlic, jalapeños, and tomato paste. Cook for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring constantly, until the tomato paste darkens slightly.
    If the mixture starts to stick or looks too dry, add a splash of water and scrape up the browned bits from the bottom of the pan.

  3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, and oregano and cook for about 30 seconds, stirring vigorously to release their aromas.

  4. Deglaze with the red wine, scraping the bottom of the pan to loosen any stuck bits. Let it simmer rapidly for 3 to 4 minutes.

  5. Add the vegetable broth, beans, cocoa powder, bay leaves, soy sauce, hand-crushed tomatoes with their juices, salt, black pepper to taste, and 1 tablespoon of maple syrup. Stir well to combine.

  6. Bring the chili to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Avoid boiling or simmering too vigorously, as this can cause the beans to break down too much.

  7. Let the chili cook for about 1½ hours, or up to 2 hours, stirring occasionally, until it becomes thick and creamy and the flavors are fully developed. Remove the bay leaves.

  8. Stir in 1 tablespoon of lime juice, the vinegar, and the coriander. Taste and adjust as needed, adding more lime juice for acidity
    and up to 2 additional tablespoons of maple syrup to balance bitterness. Season with salt and pepper if necessary.

  9. If you like, you can serve the chili with rice on the side. However, this is optional and not really recommended, as it can dilute the flavor.

  10. Serve with toppings of your choice, such as red onions, chopped coriander, sliced spring onions, vegan sour cream, avocado, tortilla chips, and more.

Last words:


Credit goes to Nisha Vora from rainbowplantlife.com for the idea for this chili. I just simplified it a little and adapted it to the European market, because many of the ingredients are difficult to find.

The mixture of the many spices completely blew my mind the first time I tried it. We're just not used to that kind of thing. But after I ate it, I couldn't get enough.
