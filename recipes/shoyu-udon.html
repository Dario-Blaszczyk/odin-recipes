This Shoyu Udon Broth warms your heart


A shoyu udon broth with shiitake mushrooms, glazed chicken, pak choi, and spring onions where simple preparation meets complex flavors.
I adapted this broth with udon noodles from a ramen recipe. It has an incredibly intense flavor,
offers plenty of variety, and is very filling. It's especially enjoyable on colder evenings and easy to prepare.
Not only is it warming and healthy, it also looks beautiful. Your guests will be delighted!


Ingredients:

For the broth:

  • 1,5 liter chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1-2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 12 shiitake mushrooms (clean them)
  • 1 mini pak choi or chinese cabbage leaves
  • 2 spring onions, roughly chopped (white for broth, green for topping)
  • 2 cloves garlic, smashed but still whole
  • About a 2cm piece of ginger, finely chopped
  • 60 ml soy sauce
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 teaspoon miso paste
  • 2 packages of fresh udon noodles
  • Sesame seeds for topping
  • Sesame oil
  • Roasted peanuts (optional)
  • 1 ½ tablespoons soy sauce

For the glazed chicken:

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoon honey
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 1-2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 boneless chicken thighs with skin, or other chicken if not available

How to make the Shoyu Udon Broth:

  1. Start by placing a small pot (or steamer) on the stove with only water in it. When the water starts to steam, place the shiitake mushrooms together with the
    individual leaves of the pak choi (or Chinese cabbage leaves) on a steamer insert and close the lid. Turn down the heat so that the water does not start to boil vigorously.

  2. Meanwhile, heat a pan on high heat. Once it has reached the desired temperature (water drop test), lower the heat
    and add the oil with the spring onions, garlic, ginger and miso paste and sauté for about 40 seconds until you can smell the aroma.

  3. Pour in the stock followed by the soy sauce and stir to combine. Bring the broth to a boil and then reduce to a simmer. Continue to simmer the sauce for 5-10 minutes.

  4. Season the broth with salt to taste. Keep warm until ready to use.

  5. Start by heating a large sauté pan over medium to high heat.

  6. As the pan warms up, combine the soy sauce, honey, and garlic in a small bowl and mix well. Set the mixture aside for later use.

  7. Pat the chicken dry with a paper towel to remove any excess moisture. Pour in enough oil to lightly coat the base of the pan and allow it to heat thoroughly.

  8. Once the oil begins to lightly smoke, add the chicken to the pan with the skin facing down. Sear for around 3 minutes, or until the skin turns golden brown.
    Turn the chicken over, lower the heat to medium, and continue cooking for another 4 to 6 minutes, until fully cooked and nicely browned on the second side.

  9. Brush the chicken with the glaze, turning it and applying more glaze until both sides are evenly coated.
    Transfer the chicken to a cooling rack set over a baking sheet and keep it there until ready to serve.

  10. Finally, fill a small pot with lightly salted water and bring it to a boil. Once it boils,
    add the udon noodles to the water and cook them according to the package instructions. Drain the water and set aside to serve.

  11. To serve, take a bowl, add a portion of the udon noodles, and fill it 2/3 full with the broth.
    Now place your steamed vegetables and glazed chicken individually on the sides. Sprinkle some of the chopped spring onions, sesame seeds, and peanuts on top.
    Finish it off with a few drops of sesame oil. If there is any glaze left in the pan from the chicken, use a spoon to drizzle it over the topping.
